# Validator Take

Validator take is the share of delegated-stake emissions that a validator keeps before the remaining
emissions flow to stakers
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The term is a distribution concept. It describes a split inside delegated-stake emissions after the
validator-side amount already exists, not the consensus step that produced that amount
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## What It Represents

Validator take concerns the portion of emissions tied to delegated stake on a selected subnet. On
netuid 1, the key relationship is between a validator and the stakers who delegated to it: the take
is the validator's kept share, and the remaining delegated-stake emissions flow to those stakers at
tempo distribution ([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's commission split. They should not be read as
representing production settings on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Subnet Owner Take

On a subnet such as netuid 1, validator take and subnet owner take name different emission slices.
Validator take is the percentage a validator keeps from delegated-stake dividends before the
remainder flows to nominators, while subnet owner take is the documented 18 percent share of subnet
alpha emissions routed to the subnet owner as stake
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Validator take** — validator–delegator split on validation dividends at tempo distribution.
- **Subnet owner take** — global owner cut from that subnet's emission bucket.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

Validator take names the commission rate on validation dividends on a subnet such as netuid 1, while
a child hotkey names the authorized key a parent delegate uses to perform validation with
re-delegated stake
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

- **Validator take** — fee applied when dividends split between validator and stakers.
- **Child hotkey** — worker identity that earns dividends under delegation.

## Distinction from Dividends

Validator take is the share of validator dividends kept by the validator hotkey. Dividends are the
validator-side reward stream produced from subnet emissions before that split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

- **Dividends** — validator-side payout category consensus produces.
- **Validator take** — configured share kept from the delegated-stake portion at distribution.

## Distinction from Childkey Take

A child hotkey carries its own take rate, the [[Childkey Take]], which applies at a different point
in the dividend flow. Validator take is the commission a validator keeps from emissions tied to
delegated stake before the remainder reaches delegators
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

- **Validator take** — split between validator and its delegators.
- **[[Childkey Take]]** — split between parent hotkey and child hotkey validating on its behalf.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Validator take is the commission on delegated-stake dividends. A validator permit is the on-chain
right to submit counting weights and take part in consensus
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

- **Validator permit** — right to submit counting weights before dividends exist.
- **Validator take** — commission applied once delegated-stake dividends are credited.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake is the combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey. Validator take is the
commission rate applied when delegated-emission returns are split at distribution
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Effective stake** — combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey.
- **Validator take** — commission rate on delegated returns at distribution.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator delegates TAO to a validator hotkey. Validator take names what share of
delegated-emission returns the validator keeps before the remainder flows to those nominators
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Nominator** — role that supplies delegated support.
- **Validator take** — configured share the validator keeps before the remainder flows to stakers.

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Validator take is the commission a validator keeps from delegated-emission returns before nominators
receive the remainder, while delegation rewards are what remains for delegated stake after that
split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Validator take** — cut taken off the top of delegated returns.
- **Delegation rewards** — portion that reaches nominators afterward.

## Distinction from Emission Split

Validator take is not a fourth headline share in the emission split. It is the cut a validator keeps
from delegated stake inside the validator-and-staker bucket after that bucket is credited
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Emission split** — role proportions credited at tempo end.
- **Validator take** — commission applied inside the validator-and-staker bucket.

## Distinction from Incentives

[Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) name miner-side emission
outcomes produced from clipped and aggregated weight signals. Validator take names the configured
share a validator keeps when the delegated-stake portion is later split
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Incentives** — miner-side emission outcomes from consensus.
- **Validator take** — validator commission on delegated-stake dividends at distribution.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Validator take is the configurable commission a validator keeps from delegated-stake emissions at
distribution. It names a post-settlement split rule inside the staking relationship, not the
consensus mechanism that builds validator dividend shares from weight submissions
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on the selected netuid. It
reads the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the
result into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Yuma produces the validator-side dividend pool from consensus; validator take applies afterward when
the delegated-stake portion is split between validator and nominators. Settlement and commission are
separate stages on the same emission path
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Readers should not read a higher take percentage as proof of stronger weight influence—that is stake
weight vocabulary. Take only governs how delegated dividends are shared after Yuma settles.

- **Validator take** — commission on delegated-stake validator emissions at distribution.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Distinction from Staking

Validator take belongs to the staking relationship between a validator and the stakers who support
that validator. Delegation describes the support relationship, while validator take describes the
validator-kept share of the delegated-stake emissions
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Delegate stake / effective stake** — stake context around a validator.
- **Validator take** — how the delegated-stake emission share is split at distribution.

## Reader Boundary

Validator take should be read as reference vocabulary for emission distribution. It is not a
recommendation, validator quality score, or complete explanation of staking outcomes
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Distinction from Delegate

Validator take and delegate name a fee and the party that charges it. A delegate is a validator that
accepts stake from nominators and validates on their behalf, while validator take is the percentage
that delegate keeps from the rewards generated on delegated stake before the remainder is paid to
nominators
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegate** — a validator that accepts stake from nominators.
- **Validator take** — the commission that delegate keeps from delegated-stake rewards.

## Further Reading

- [Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)

# Stake Weight

Stake weight is the subnet-level stake measure used to describe validator influence in Bittensor
consensus. It is the computed stake value tied to consensus power and emissions inside a subnet
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

The term links staking vocabulary to consensus evaluation. It answers how much influence a submitted
evaluation carries after stake context is applied, while validator weights answer what was signaled
about miner work.

## Distinction from Validator Weights

Validator weights are evaluation signals a validator assigns to miners. Stake weight is the computed
stake value that scales how strongly that validator's signal counts in Yuma Consensus
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Weights vocabulary names what was scored; stake weight vocabulary names influence around that score.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake names total TAO staked behind a validator, including delegated stake
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

Stake weight is the subnet consensus influence measure derived from stake components on that subnet
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

Backing totals and consensus influence answer different reader questions on the staking path.

## Distinction from Relative Stake Weight

Stake weight is the absolute consensus influence measure for a validator on a subnet. Relative stake
weight compares one validator's stake weight to others on the same subnet
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: Relative Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#relative-stake-weight)).

- **Stake weight** — absolute consensus influence derived from stake behind a validator.
- **Relative stake weight** — comparison of one validator's weight to others on the subnet.

Absolute influence vocabulary and comparative ranking vocabulary answer different questions
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from Validator Trust

Stake weight names consensus influence derived from stake behind a validator position. Validator
trust measures how aligned a validator's submitted weights were with the consensus result
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: Validator Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-trust)).

- **Stake weight** — stake-derived influence on how validator signals count.
- **Validator trust** — alignment measure for submitted weights against consensus.

Influence vocabulary names counting power; trust vocabulary names evaluation alignment
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator

Stake weight and the blockchain-versus-subnet-validator comparison clarify which validator role
stake weight applies to. A blockchain validator secures the chain at the network layer, while a
subnet validator evaluates miners inside a subnet, and stake weight is the value that scales a
subnet validator's consensus influence, not the block-production duty of a blockchain validator
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator),
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Blockchain validator vs subnet validator** — separates network-layer block production from
  subnet evaluation roles.
- **Stake weight** — subnet-validator consensus influence, not a blockchain-validator duty.

## Distinction from Conviction and Locked Stake

Stake weight names the influence measure derived from staked value. Conviction and locked stake name
the separate time-based commitment signal produced by locking already-staked alpha
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

- **Stake weight** — computed consensus influence from staked value.
- **Conviction and locked stake** — time-based commitment from locking staked alpha.

## Distinction from Max Validators

Max validators is the hyperparameter ceiling on how many validator permits a subnet can issue. Stake
weight is the computed influence measure used to rank validators for those permit slots
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

- **Max validators** — configured cap on validator permits.
- **Stake weight** — computed influence that ranks who receives permits.

The hyperparameter sets the ceiling; stake-weight ranking fills permit slots up to that maximum
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Stake weight is the computed influence that scales how strongly a validator's submitted weights
count inside Yuma aggregation. It names stake-backed weighting input, not the full mechanism that
credits emission shares at the epoch boundary
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: TAO Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-weight)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that reads the weight matrix, applies stake weighting,
clipping, and bonding, and then credits miner incentives and validator dividends on the selected
netuid ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Weight Matrix](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The validator permit section above covers who may submit counting weights. Stake-weight vocabulary
names how much influence those submissions carry; Yuma vocabulary names the full settlement pass
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Readers should not read stake weight as a payout quote or miner rank label. It scales validator
influence inside Yuma; credited outcomes still depend on the pass on the relevant netuid
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Stake weight** — stake-backed influence behind validator weight signals.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain mechanism that uses validator weights to determine emission shares
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Reader Boundary

Stake weight should not be read as a payout amount, a validator trust score by itself, or proof that
a miner received a specific reward. It names validator influence inside subnet consensus—the
computed stake value that scales how strongly a submitted evaluation counts
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Validator weights say what a validator signaled about miner work. Stake weight explains how strongly
that signal participates when Yuma Consensus filters and combines validator submissions
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector)).

## Distinction from TAO Weight

Stake weight and TAO weight relate as a computed result and a coefficient that feeds it. Stake
weight is the total consensus influence a validator's stake carries, combining root TAO and subnet
alpha, while TAO weight is the global coefficient that scales the root TAO portion relative to alpha
stake when that stake weight is calculated
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: TAO Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-weight),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Stake weight** — a validator's total stake-based consensus influence.
- **TAO weight** — the coefficient scaling root TAO against alpha when computing it.

# Effective Stake

Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate. It combines the delegate's own
staked TAO with TAO delegated by nominators
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The term is amount vocabulary inside the staking model. It is not a separate asset, a new role, or a
validator-quality score.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking names the process of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey, while effective stake
names the combined total of delegate-owned and nominated TAO behind that hotkey after support is
recorded ([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind that hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation names how nominated TAO becomes attached to a validator hotkey, while effective stake
names the combined total of delegate-owned and nominated TAO behind that hotkey
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation** — nominated TAO attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind that hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

For netuid 1 examples, effective stake and delegate stake name different figures behind the same
validator hotkey. Effective stake is the combined total of delegate-owned and nominator-delegated
TAO behind that hotkey, while delegate stake names only the TAO the delegate stakes personally
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Effective stake** — combined self-stake plus nominated support behind the hotkey.
- **Delegate stake** — validator self-staked TAO only.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Effective stake and validator take describe a delegate's economics as amount and split. Effective
stake is the total TAO associated with a delegate after self-stake and nominator-delegated stake are
counted, while validator take is the commission the delegate keeps from emissions earned on
delegated stake before the remainder flows to nominators
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Effective stake** — combined backing total behind the delegate hotkey.
- **Validator take** — commission on delegated-stake returns at distribution.

## Distinction from Delegated Stake

Effective stake and delegated stake answer different questions about support behind a validator
hotkey. Delegated stake names TAO nominators attach to a delegate, while effective stake names the
combined total after that nominated support is added to the delegate's own stake
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

- **Delegated stake** — nominator-supplied TAO attached to the delegate.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is a participant role that delegates TAO to a validator hotkey. Effective stake is the
combined total recorded behind that hotkey after nomination is included
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Nominator** — participant role that delegates TAO to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined total recorded behind that hotkey after nomination.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Autostaking routes miner emissions into delegated stake automatically. Effective stake is the
combined total behind a delegate hotkey that those routed amounts can increase over time
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Autostaking** — automatic routing of miner emissions into validator stake.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

Routing vocabulary names how emissions become stake; effective-stake vocabulary names the measured
total ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Dividends

Dividends are validator-side emission outcomes after consensus processing. Effective stake remains
the combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey before payout vocabulary enters the discussion
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Dividends** — validator-side emission outcomes after consensus processing.
- **Effective stake** — combined staking total behind the hotkey before payout vocabulary.

## Distinction from Hotkey-Coldkey Pair

Effective stake and the hotkey-coldkey pair describe different parts of the same staking picture.
Effective stake is the aggregate stake a validator's hotkey carries across its own and all delegated
stake, while the hotkey-coldkey pair is the authentication relationship between the operational
hotkey and the ownership coldkey under which that validator acts
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — ownership-and-operation link for one wallet identity.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Effective stake and stake weight are related but different delegate terms. Effective stake names the
total staked support behind a delegate after self-stake and nomination are combined, while stake
weight names how strongly that delegate's signals count when validator inputs are merged in Yuma
Consensus
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Effective stake** — combined staked support behind a delegate hotkey.
- **Stake weight** — computed influence used in subnet consensus processing.

## Distinction from Transferring Stake

Transferring stake and effective stake separate ownership from validator backing totals.
Transferring stake is the operation that changes which coldkey owns a staked position while leaving
it on the same hotkey, whereas effective stake is the combined delegate-owned plus nominated total
associated with the validator that position backs
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview: Transferring stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#transferring-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

Transferring ownership does not move effective stake between validators the way moving stake does.

- **Transferring stake** — changing the coldkey that owns a position on the same validator.
- **Effective stake** — combined backing total behind the validator hotkey.

## Distinction from Alpha Staker

An alpha staker holds subnet alpha by staking to a validator on that subnet. Effective stake is the
validator's combined self-and-delegated total that those positions feed
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

- **Alpha staker** — participant holding subnet alpha through validator staking.
- **Effective stake** — combined backing total behind the validator hotkey.

Holder vocabulary names who supplies alpha; effective-stake vocabulary names the validator total
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Effective stake names the combined staking total behind a validator on one subnet. Yuma Consensus
settles stake-weighted validator submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Effective stake** — combined self-and-delegated stake backing a validator.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from weighted submissions to emission shares.

Amount vocabulary names backing behind a delegate; Yuma vocabulary names reward settlement
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

## Reader Boundary

Effective stake should be read as a delegate-associated staked TAO total, not as stake weight,
validator weights, rank, trust, dividends, or validator-selection advice
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

Use effective stake when the focus is the combined total behind a delegate. Use delegate stake for
the delegate's own stake, delegated stake for nominator-provided stake, and stake weight for subnet
consensus influence
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Stake

Stake and effective stake describe a raw amount and an aggregated influence figure. Stake is TAO
committed to a hotkey, while effective stake is a validator's combined self-and-delegated total that
the protocol uses to weigh its consensus influence, so individual stake contributions sum into the
effective stake of the validator they back
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Stake** — TAO committed to a hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — the validator's combined self-and-delegated total that sets influence.

# Delegation Rewards

Delegation rewards are what a delegator earns for staking TAO to a validator instead of validating
itself. By backing a validator with stake, a delegator shares in the rewards that validator
produces, in proportion to how much stake it contributed
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## What It Represents

A delegator's reward is its proportional slice of validator-side returns, scaled by stake relative
to everything else delegated to the same validator. Before delegators are paid, the validator keeps
a take on the delegated portion, so delegation rewards are net of that commission at tempo
distribution
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

On netuid 1, examples illustrate one subnet's delegator return path. They should not be read as
representing production returns on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Autostaking

Delegation rewards are the proportional return delegated stake earns after distribution and take.
Autostaking automatically routes miner emissions into a validator-staking position
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation rewards** — proportional return delegated stake earns after distribution and take.
- **Autostaking** — routing path that can grow the delegated position over time.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Validator take is the commission a validator keeps from delegated-emission returns before nominators
receive the remainder. Delegation rewards are what remains for delegated stake after that split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Validator take** — commission kept from delegated returns before the nominator split.
- **Delegation rewards** — proportional share that remains for delegated stake.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is the participant role that delegates support to a validator. Delegation rewards are
the proportional returns that role earns on delegated stake after distribution
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Nominator** — role that supplies delegated support.
- **Delegation rewards** — proportional returns that role earns after distribution.

## Distinction from Nominate

Nominate is the action of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey. Delegation rewards are the
returns that attached support can earn after consensus distribution and take
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Nominate** — action of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Delegation rewards** — returns once distribution and take run at tempo end.

## Distinction from Dividends

Dividends are the validator-side emission outcomes Yuma Consensus produces from bonds and miner
incentives. Delegation rewards are the proportional share nominators receive after validator take is
applied to the delegated portion
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Dividends** — validator-side emission outcomes from consensus.
- **Delegation rewards** — nominator share after validator take on delegated emissions.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake is the combined total of delegate-owned and nominator-delegated TAO behind a
validator hotkey. Delegation rewards are the proportional returns delegated stake earns after
distribution and take
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Effective stake** — combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey.
- **Delegation rewards** — outcome vocabulary for what that support earns after take.

## Distinction from Liquidity Provider Rewards

Delegation rewards are a share of a validator's emissions earned by delegating stake to that
validator. Liquidity provider rewards are fee income from trades crossing a provided liquidity range
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

- **Delegation rewards** — share of validator emissions for delegated stake.
- **Liquidity provider rewards** — fee income from trades crossing a liquidity range.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking is the process of attaching TAO support to a validator. Delegation rewards are the share a
delegator earns from that validator's emissions for providing the support
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — process of attaching TAO support to a validator.
- **Delegation rewards** — share earned from that validator's emissions after take.

## Distinction from Emission

Emission names the broader subnet reward and injection flow. Delegation rewards name the nominator's
proportional share of validator-side returns after distribution and take on delegated stake
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Emission** — broader subnet reward and injection flow.
- **Delegation rewards** — nominator share inside one validator relationship.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Delegation rewards name the returns that flow to nominators who stake TAO to subnet validators after
distribution and validator take. They describe a delegator outcome inside one staking relationship,
not the consensus mechanism that builds emission shares from weight submissions
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary. It reads the weight matrix
from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result into miner
incentives and validator dividend shares that fund downstream delegation flows
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate delegation rewards from staking, emission, and validator take. Yuma
settles the validator-side pool from consensus; delegation rewards describe how nominators share
that pool after take—not how weights were aggregated
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Readers should treat Yuma as the settlement input to validator dividends, and delegation rewards as
the delegator-facing share of what remains after take on delegated stake.

- **Delegation rewards** — nominator returns after validator-side distribution and take.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level and is not financial advice. What a delegator actually
earns depends on the validator's performance, the take it sets, and the live distribution of stake
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation and delegation rewards relate as an arrangement and the income it produces. Delegation is
the act of assigning stake to a validator you do not operate, while delegation rewards are the
ongoing emissions that arrangement pays the delegator, after the validator's take is deducted
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Delegation** — the act of assigning stake to a validator you do not operate.
- **Delegation rewards** — the emissions that delegation pays, net of validator take.

# Validator Take

Validator take is the share of delegated-stake emissions that a validator keeps before the remaining
emissions flow to stakers
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The term is a distribution concept. It describes a split inside delegated-stake emissions after the
validator-side amount already exists, not the consensus step that produced that amount
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Validator Take Is Not Stake Weight

Validator take is a distribution split. It tells you how validator-side emissions or dividends tied
to delegated stake are shared between the validator and its nominators after that validator-side
amount already exists
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Stake weight is different vocabulary. It is the computed stake-based influence that scales how
strongly a validator's submitted weights count in subnet consensus, not the fee split that later
shapes [[Delegation Rewards]]
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

If the question is "how much backing sits behind this validator," see [[Effective Stake]]. If the
question is "how much influence does this validator have in consensus," use stake weight and
[[Yuma Consensus]]. Validator take does not answer either question.

## What It Represents

Validator take concerns the portion of emissions tied to delegated stake on a selected subnet. On
netuid 1, the key relationship is between a validator and the stakers who delegated to it: the take
is the validator's kept share, and the remaining delegated-stake emissions flow to those stakers at
tempo distribution ([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's commission split. They should not be read as
representing production settings on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Subnet Owner Take

On a subnet such as netuid 1, validator take and subnet owner take name different emission slices.
Validator take is the percentage a validator keeps from delegated-stake dividends before the
remainder flows to nominators, while subnet owner take is the documented 18 percent share of subnet
alpha emissions routed to the subnet owner as stake
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Validator take** — validator–delegator split on validation dividends at tempo distribution.
- **Subnet owner take** — global owner cut from that subnet's emission bucket.
