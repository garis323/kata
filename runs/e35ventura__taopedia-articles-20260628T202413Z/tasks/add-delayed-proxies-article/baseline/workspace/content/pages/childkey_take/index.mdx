# Childkey Take

Childkey take, called the child hotkey take in Bittensor's
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys) documentation, is the
subnet-specific share of resulting dividends a child hotkey keeps when it validates on behalf of a
parent hotkey. The documented take range is `0` (0%) to `0.18` (18%), with a default of `0`
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

The term belongs to delegated validation on a selected
[netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid). It does not name the child
hotkey itself; it names the rate applied to the parent-child dividend split on that subnet
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

## What It Represents

A parent hotkey can re-delegate a portion of its stake to a child hotkey so the child validates on
that parent's behalf on one subnet. The childkey take is the portion of the resulting dividends the
child keeps for doing that validation work
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

That makes childkey take a payout-split term inside a parent-child validation relationship. It is
not a separate registration status, a weight score, or a general validator commission applied across
every subnet.

## Subnet-Specific Rate

The child-hotkeys documentation states that the take is subnet-specific: one child hotkey can have
one take on one netuid and a different take on another
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid)).

Readers should therefore attach the netuid before comparing childkey-take statements. The stable
concept is the per-subnet rate; the live value belongs to the selected subnet configuration rather
than to the child hotkey in the abstract.

## Change Window

The same documentation states that a given child hotkey's take rate can only be adjusted once per 30
days ([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

This cooldown is about how often the rate may be edited. It does not change what childkey take
means; it only limits how frequently the configured percentage can be updated on chain.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Childkey take is the share a child hotkey keeps when validating on behalf of a parent hotkey on one
subnet. Validator take is the separate commission a validator keeps from delegated-stake emissions
before the remainder flows to nominators
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Childkey take** — parent/child split on routed validation dividends for one subnet.
- **Validator take** — validator/nominator commission on delegated-stake emissions.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

Child hotkeys name the authorized worker keys a parent hotkey assigns per subnet. Childkey take
names the rate attached to that relationship
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

- **Child hotkey** — the key that validates on the parent's behalf.
- **Childkey take** — the percentage that key keeps from the resulting dividends.

## Distinction from Subnet Owner Take

Childkey take arises only when a parent hotkey re-delegates stake to a child hotkey for validation.
Subnet owner take is the documented protocol share of subnet alpha emissions allocated to the subnet
owner ([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Childkey take** — optional payout split inside parent-child validation.
- **Subnet owner take** — protocol allocation to the subnet owner.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the term. It does not report the current childkey-take setting for a particular
hotkey or subnet. Those are live chain-state or operator-configuration questions that should be
checked for the specific netuid involved
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid)).
